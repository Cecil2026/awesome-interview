# Interview Roadmaps

Timeline-based study plans for your next software engineering interview. Pick a plan, follow it week by week, and check items off as you go. Every roadmap assumes you already know how to write code in at least one language — these plans tune your existing skills toward interview performance, not from-scratch CS education.

## The three plans

| Plan | Length | Best for |
|------|--------|----------|
| [Frontend Engineer](./frontend-engineer.md) | 8 weeks | UI engineers targeting product-facing roles (React, Vue, Angular). Balanced algorithm prep, framework internals, and UI system design. |
| [Backend Engineer](./backend-engineer.md) | 8 weeks | Server engineers targeting infrastructure, API, or platform roles. Algorithm-heavy with deep system design and distributed systems coverage. |
| [Fullstack Engineer](./fullstack-engineer.md) | 10 weeks | Generalists applying to roles that interview on both halves of the stack. Longer plan because the surface area is larger. |

## How to pick a plan

Ask yourself which of these matches your target role:

- **The job description spends more time on UI frameworks, accessibility, browser APIs, and design systems** → Frontend.
- **The job description spends more time on services, databases, scalability, queues, or "design a system that handles N requests per second"** → Backend.
- **The job description treats frontend and backend as equal, or you're applying to a startup that expects both** → Fullstack.

If you're applying to multiple role types, pick the plan that matches your weaker side. You're already going to perform well on your strong side; the prep should move the floor.

## How to use the plans

1. Read the entire roadmap end to end before you start. Don't surprise yourself with week 6 in week 6.
2. Block time on your calendar. Treat the weekday and weekend hours as appointments, not suggestions.
3. Track problem counts. The numbers in each week (e.g., "10 easy array problems") are the minimum, not the ceiling. If you finish early, do more in the same category — don't skip ahead.
4. Hit the weekly milestone before moving on. If you can't, repeat the week instead of advancing with gaps.
5. Use the universal [checklist](./checklist.md) starting two weeks before your first onsite.

## Time commitment at a glance

| Plan | Weekday/day | Weekend/day | Total |
|------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Frontend (8 wk) | 1-2 hr | 4-6 hr | 80-100 hr |
| Backend (8 wk) | 1.5-2 hr | 5-7 hr | 100-120 hr |
| Fullstack (10 wk) | 1.5-2 hr | 5-7 hr | 120-150 hr |

If you cannot commit this time, use the "If you have less time" section at the bottom of each plan. A compressed plan honestly executed beats a full plan abandoned in week 3.

## The universal checklist

Regardless of plan, use [checklist.md](./checklist.md) for:

- 2-weeks-before logistics (mocks, format confirmation, questions to ask)
- 1-week-before taper (no new material)
- Day-before prep (setup, STAR review, sleep)
- Day-of routine (timing, snacks, breathing)
- Post-interview debrief (what they asked, what to improve, thank-you email)

## Conventions used in every roadmap

- Every week has a one-line **Focus** statement.
- Algorithm work is grouped by **pattern** (arrays, trees, DP, etc.), not by individual problem. Use the LeetCode top-100 lists, Blind 75, or NeetCode 150 as the source list.
- Resources are cited by **name only** (book titles, courses, blog series). Look them up — the canonical sources are easy to find.
- Behavioral prep is woven through every plan, not crammed at the end. Interviewers can tell when you wrote your stories the night before.
- Each week ends with a **Milestone** — a specific, checkable outcome. If you can't hit it, you're not done with the week.

## What these plans deliberately skip

- Language tutorials. If you can't write a for-loop fluently, do that first.
- Project portfolio building. Useful, but not interview prep.
- Resume and recruiter outreach. Important, but covered elsewhere in this repo.
- Negotiation. Also covered elsewhere.

Start with your plan. Good luck.
